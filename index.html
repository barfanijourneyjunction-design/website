Sacred Places

The ChĀr DhĀm

Four sacred shrines nestled in the majestic Himalayas, forming the holiest pilgrimage circuit in Hinduism.

🛕

BadrĪnĀth

Chamolī, Uttarākhaṇḍ • 3,133m

Dedicated to Lord Viṣṇu, Badrīnāth Temple is one of the most sacred temples in India. Set between the Nar and Narayana mountain ranges along the Alaknandā River, this ancient shrine is believed to be where Lord Viṣṇu meditated.

Season: May - November
🏔

KedĀrnĀth

Rudraprayāg, Uttarākhaṇḍ • 3,583m

The highest among the 12 Jyotirliṅgas, Kedārnāth Temple is dedicated to Lord Śiva. Located near the Mandākinī River and surrounded by snow-capped peaks, this 1000-year-old temple stands as a testament to devotion and resilience.

Season: May - October
🌊

GaṄgotrĪ

Uttarkāśī, Uttarākhaṇḍ • 3,100m

The origin of the sacred River Gaṅgā, Gaṅgotrī is where Goddess Gaṅgā descended to Earth. The temple dedicated to Goddess Gaṅgā sits on the banks of the Bhāgīrathī River, surrounded by towering cedar and pine forests.

Season: May - October
✨

YamunotrĪ

Uttarkāśī, Uttarākhaṇḍ • 3,293m

The source of River Yamunā and seat of Goddess Yamunā. The temple sits near the Champasar Glacier and features hot water springs including the famous Sūrya Kuṇḍ, where devotees cook rice and potatoes as prasād offerings.

Season: May - October
